# Session lesson: delivery readiness, product philosophy, and broadened collaboration forms

Use this when iterating a public HTML/PDF-first strategic proposal under time pressure, especially when the user asks whether the artifact is ready to deliver.

## What happened

During an HTML-first AI sales-operations proposal, the user corrected several directions:

- A workbench visual that only showed lead search narrowed the concept too much. The system needed to show the whole chain: multi-platform discovery, lightweight outreach, controlled collaboration/private-domain management, AI/human handoff, partner conversion, and feedback/productization.
- A specific “small group” mechanism was only one possible collaboration form. The document needed broader operating forms rather than locking onto one tactic.
- The user's confirmed philosophical concepts were “全 AI 自动化” and “自举分形”; these needed to become the proposal's high-level spine, not side notes.
- Before deciding whether to deliver, the agent needed to review the project constraints/concept files and report: progress, remaining gaps, and whether the artifact could be delivered tonight.

## Durable pattern

For strategic/product deliverables, distinguish:

1. **Operating philosophy** — e.g. “全 AI 自动化” should be framed as human-position-up automation, not unattended automation or AI impersonation.
   - AI handles repeatable, structured, replayable, auditable information work.
   - Humans handle trust, judgment, negotiation, relationships, and key conversion.
   - Explicitly state the boundary: not pretending to be human; can be taken over, replayed, and audited.

2. **Product philosophy** — e.g. “自举分形” should be treated as a memorable product thesis.
   - Use the system to sell the current product.
   - Use real sales operations to train and shape the system.
   - Turn the system into a product layer that can sell more products.
   - Express the fractal idea as repeated structure across scales: one conversation -> FAQ; one lead flow -> Agent; one channel partner -> partner network; one internal tool -> external product.

3. **Collaboration forms** — do not reduce the operating system to one communication tactic.
   Useful public-safe patterns include:
   - 单客协作舱 — one high-value customer/partner operating space.
   - 伙伴推进室 — partner enablement room with materials, scripts, FAQ, follow-up rhythm.
   - 共创训练营 — task-based screening and growth space for high-agency individuals.
   - 场景实验室 — focused experiments around vertical problems such as customer-service peaks or channel conversion.
   - IM 聚合收件箱 — unified summary/reminder/archive/takeover layer over scattered conversations.
   - 影子助手模式 — AI stays behind the human, drafting replies and surfacing risks for high-cognition targets.

## Delivery readiness review checklist

When the user asks “can this be delivered tonight?” or equivalent:

1. Re-read or summarize the authoritative project contract: mission, product facts/open questions, report-production policy, design system, recovery state, validation report.
2. Do one final public-copy pass:
   - Remove internal labels, fixed timelines, unverified prices/cases/policies, personal backstory, and sensitive distribution wording.
   - Ensure the key thesis is visible in the hero / first sections.
   - Convert open-ended “should we discuss?” wording into action-oriented “post-delivery alignment points.”
3. Validate static delivery hygiene:
   - self-contained HTML if it may be shared standalone;
   - no links/images/scripts unless intentionally needed;
   - no hover/transition/animation/keyframes for PDF-first static reports;
   - no public leakage tokens such as workdir paths, draft labels, or internal constraints.
4. Separate **content readiness** from **export/toolchain readiness**.
   - If HTML is content-ready but PDF export is blocked by browser/toolchain setup, say so explicitly.
   - Recommend delivering the self-contained HTML now and exporting PDF later, rather than delaying the decision because of a tooling issue.
5. Final report shape:
   - what changed this round;
   - which constraints/files were reviewed;
   - remaining gaps;
   - delivery decision: ready / ready with caveats / not ready;
   - recommended next action under the user's time constraint.

## Public wording pitfalls

- Avoid making “全 AI 自动化” sound like no humans are involved. Use “人类位置上移.”
- Avoid narrowing collaboration to “small groups” or one private-domain tactic. Use “可控协作场域” or a family of collaboration forms.
- Avoid sensitive commercial phrasing such as “多级分销.” Prefer “伙伴网络,” “转介绍,” “渠道递推,” “渠道协作.”
- Avoid weak internal phrasing such as “讨论版,” “static HTML,” “暂不做什么,” “10 页以内,” or “一次性讲成大平台” in public deliverables.
